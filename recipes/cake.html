Pineapple-Upside Down Cake

Indulge in the tropical delight of our Pineapple Upside-Down Cake! This classic dessert features caramelized pineapple slices and cherries, beautifully baked into a soft, buttery cake. Each slice offers a perfect balance of sweetness and a hint of tanginess. Easy to make and a joy to share, it's the ultimate treat for any occasion!

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup Butter
  • 1 cup Packed Brown Sugar
  • 20 oz Pineapple Slices in juice, drained, juice reserved
  • 1 jar Marashino Cheeries
  • 1 box Betty Crocker Super Moist Yellow Cake Mix
  • Vegetable Oil and Eggs called for in cake mix box

Steps:

  1. Heat oven to 350°F (325°F for dark or nonstick pan). Melt butter in 13x9-inch pan in oven. Sprinkle brown sugar evenly over melted butter. Arrange pineapple slices on brown sugar. Place cherry in center of each pineapple slice, and arrange remaining cherries around slices; press gently into brown sugar.
  2. Add enough water to reserved pineapple juice to measure 1 cup. Make cake batter as directed on box, substituting pineapple juice mixture for the water. Pour batter over pineapple and cherries.
  3. Bake 40 to 46 minutes (44 to 53 minutes for dark or nonstick pan) or until toothpick inserted in center comes out clean. Immediately run knife around side of pan to loosen cake. Place heatproof serving plate upside down onto pan; turn plate and pan over. Leave pan over cake 5 minutes so brown sugar topping can drizzle over cake; remove pan. Cool 30 minutes. Serve warm or cool. Store covered in refrigerator.
